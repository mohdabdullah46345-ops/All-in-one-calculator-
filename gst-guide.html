GST Explained: How to Calculate GST, CGST & SGST in India

By Abdullah · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

GST stands for Goods and Services Tax. It is a single, unified indirect tax that applies to the supply of most goods and services in India. Introduced in 2017, GST replaced a tangle of older taxes such as VAT, service tax, and excise duty with one streamlined system. For shoppers, business owners, and freelancers alike, understanding how GST is calculated helps you read invoices correctly and price your products fairly.

The common GST slabs

Goods and services are placed into different tax brackets, commonly known as slabs. The widely used rates are:

  • 0% — essential items such as many fresh foods.
  • 5% — common household necessities.
  • 12% — processed foods and certain goods.
  • 18% — the most common slab, covering many services and electronics.
  • 28% — luxury and premium items.

The exact slab for a product can change as per government notifications, so always confirm the current rate for your specific item.

CGST, SGST and IGST — what is the difference?

The total GST you pay is usually split into two parts:

  • CGST (Central GST): the share collected by the central government.
  • SGST (State GST): the share collected by the state government.

For a sale within the same state, the GST is divided equally between CGST and SGST. For example, an 18% GST becomes 9% CGST + 9% SGST. When goods or services move between two different states, a single IGST (Integrated GST) is charged instead, which the central government later shares with the states.

How to add GST (GST-exclusive amount)

When a price is quoted before tax, you add GST on top:

GST Amount = Base × (Rate ÷ 100)
Total = Base + GST Amount

Example: For a base price of ₹10,000 at 18% GST, the tax is ₹1,800 and the total payable is ₹11,800 (split as ₹900 CGST + ₹900 SGST).

How to remove GST (GST-inclusive amount)

When a price already includes GST and you want to find the base amount:

Base = Total ÷ (1 + Rate ÷ 100)
GST Amount = Total − Base

Example: If a bill of ₹11,800 includes 18% GST, the base amount is ₹10,000 and the GST portion is ₹1,800. Our GST Calculator handles both "add" and "remove" modes and shows the CGST + SGST split automatically.

Who needs to understand GST?

  • Business owners who must charge and report GST correctly.
  • Freelancers and service providers who issue invoices.
  • Shoppers who want to verify the tax on their bills.
  • Students and accountants learning indirect taxation.

Frequently asked questions

Is GST added to every product?

No. Some essential goods are taxed at 0% or are exempt. The rate depends on the category of the product or service.

What is the difference between inclusive and exclusive GST?

An exclusive price is shown before GST and tax is added on top. An inclusive price already contains GST, so the tax is calculated backward from the total.
